The challenge

Hiring is slow, expensive, and never quite in time

When the roadmap accelerates, the team rarely keeps pace. Recruiting senior engineers takes months, the right niche skills are hard to find, and once headcount is fixed it's painful to flex it up for a push or down after a launch. Meanwhile deadlines slip and the work piles up on the people you already have. By the time a new hire has finally ramped up on your codebase, the moment you needed them for has often already passed.

What slows teams down

  • Long hiring cycles

    Months of sourcing and interviews before anyone writes code.

  • Niche skill gaps

    The exact expertise you need is scarce and hard to recruit.

  • Fixed headcount

    Hard to scale up for a push — or down once it's shipped.

  • Ramp-up time

    New hires take weeks to become productive on your stack.

FAQ

Dedicated teams questions

Staff augmentation vs a dedicated team — what's the difference?

With staff augmentation, individual specialists join your existing team and report into your managers, filling specific skill gaps. A dedicated team is a self-contained, cross-functional squad — engineers plus the roles around them — that owns a body of work end to end. Augmentation is ideal when you have a process and need extra hands; a dedicated pod is better when you want a whole workstream delivered.

How quickly can a team be onboarded?

After we agree on the roles and skills you need, we match candidates from our talent pool, you interview the shortlist, and approved members begin onboarding. Timelines depend on the seniority and niche of the roles, but because we draw from people we already know, we can usually move faster than a traditional hiring cycle.

Who manages the team day to day?

It's your choice. With staff augmentation you direct the work and our people follow your processes and tooling. With a managed dedicated pod, we provide a lead or delivery manager who runs the squad day to day and reports progress to you. Either way, you keep full visibility and control over priorities.

How do you handle time zones and communication?

We agree on working-hour overlap up front and adapt our schedule so there is reliable real-time collaboration with your team. Our people work in your tools — your chat, issue tracker, repos, and standups — and communicate in English, so they operate as an extension of your team rather than an outside vendor.
