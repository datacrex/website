FAQ

Cloud & DevSecOps questions

Can you reduce our cloud costs?

Often, yes. We start with a cost review to find idle resources, oversized instances, and inefficient architecture, then apply right-sizing, autoscaling, and reserved or spot capacity where it fits. Because everything is defined as code, the savings hold over time instead of drifting back up.

Which cloud should we use — AWS, Azure, or GCP?

It depends on your existing stack, team skills, compliance needs, and the managed services you rely on. We're cloud-agnostic and design for all three, so we recommend the platform that fits your workloads and budget rather than defaulting to one vendor.

What is DevSecOps?

DevSecOps means building security into your development and deployment pipeline from the start, rather than bolting it on at the end. It combines automated testing, dependency and image scanning, secrets management, and policy checks inside CI/CD so issues are caught before they reach production.

Will there be downtime during migration?

We plan migrations to minimize or avoid downtime, using approaches like phased cutovers, blue-green deployments, and data replication so the old and new environments run in parallel until you're ready to switch. The exact approach depends on your workloads, and we agree the plan with you before any change.
